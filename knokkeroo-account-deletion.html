knokkeroo Account Deletion

Request deletion or restriction of a knokkeroo account linked to a mobile phone number.

Delete your account in the app if possible

Account deletion is safest and fastest from inside the knokkeroo app.

If you can still access the app, please open Me → Delete account and complete deletion there. In-app deletion is the preferred method because it confirms that the request comes from an authenticated user.

In-app deletion permanently removes your profile, your check-in history, and your contact connections in both directions, and frees your phone number for future sign-up.

Use this form only if you cannot delete in the app

If you cannot access the app, you may submit the form below to request deletion or restriction of a knokkeroo account linked to a mobile phone number.

Submitting this form does not guarantee immediate deletion. For privacy and security reasons, we do not delete an account merely because someone submits a phone number.

We may refuse, delay, restrict, or take no action on a request if we cannot reasonably verify that the requester is the account holder, currently controls the relevant mobile phone number, or is legally authorised to act for the account holder.

knokkeroo cannot cancel, deactivate, transfer, release, or reassign your mobile phone number with your network provider. This page concerns only the deletion or restriction of a knokkeroo account linked to a phone number.

What to include in your request

Please provide only the information needed for the request:

  • The mobile phone number linked to the knokkeroo account.
  • Your contact email address for follow-up.
  • Whether you are the account holder, currently control the number, are legally authorised to act for the account holder, or are making another type of request.
  • A short explanation if you cannot delete the account inside the app.

Please do not upload or send identity documents, death certificates, legal documents, SIM documents, or other sensitive documents unless we specifically request them.

If exceptional verification is required, we will tell you what is needed. Any such information will be used only to assess the request and will not be kept longer than reasonably necessary, except where limited retention is required for security, legal compliance, dispute handling, abuse prevention, or audit purposes.

Required confirmation

Before submitting the form, you must confirm the following:

I confirm that I am the knokkeroo account holder, currently control the mobile phone number submitted, or am legally authorised to act for the account holder. I understand that false, misleading, malicious, or unauthorised requests may be refused and may result in suspension, investigation, cost recovery, civil action, or referral to relevant authorities where lawful.

By submitting the form, you confirm that the information provided is true and that the request is not false, malicious, unauthorised, or intended to interfere with another person's account, data, contacts, or use of the service.

Deceased account holders

If the account holder is deceased, a family member, executor, estate representative, or other authorised person may submit a request. We may require reasonable verification before deleting, restricting, anonymising, or otherwise acting on the account.

We will not disclose unnecessary account information during this process. In some cases, we may restrict an account, sever old connections, anonymise records, or separate a phone number from old account records rather than immediately deleting every record.

Phone-number reuse

Mobile phone numbers may be reassigned, recycled, transferred, ported, cancelled, or used by a different person over time. Control of a phone number today does not necessarily prove that the current holder is the original knokkeroo account holder.

Where a number appears to have been reassigned or where account ownership is unclear, we may restrict access, require re-verification, separate the phone number from old account records, remove stale connections, delete or anonymise records, or treat later use of that phone number as a new account.
