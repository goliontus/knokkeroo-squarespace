knokkeroo — Tester Scenarios

Thanks for testing knokkeroo! Please work through the steps below on your own phone and tick each one off. After each step, check whether what happened matches the “Expected” note. If anything is different, confusing, or broken, email us — that feedback is exactly what helps.

Before you start

You will need your phone and the knokkeroo app installed (see the beta-testing page if you have not installed it yet). A few steps work best with a second person who also has knokkeroo — a friend or family member who is testing with you — so you can connect to each other. There is no harm in trying anything here; you can always change or undo it afterwards.

1. Sign in with your number

  • Open knokkeroo. Choose your country, enter your mobile number, and tap Send verification code.
    Expected: a verification code arrives by message within a minute or so.
  • Enter the code and continue. If it is your first time, enter your name when asked.
    Expected: you reach the main screen with a Me tile and an ＋ Add Contact tile. No password is ever needed.

2. Tap knok

  • Tap the big Knok tile.
    Expected: a short flash, a little buzz and a double-knock sound; your Me tile turns/stays green and its time updates to now.
  • Leave the app, wait a moment, and come back; also try pulling down to refresh.
    Expected: the screen refreshes and you should not be left wondering whether your knok was recorded.

3. Your settings

  • Tap Me → change your name and your knok interval (how often you are expected to check in) → Save changes.
    Expected: the change saves and stays; if you try to leave with unsaved changes, the app asks whether to save or discard.

4. Quiet hours

  • In Me, turn Quiet hours on and set a sleep and wake time → Save.
    Expected: the summary shows your window (e.g. “22:00 to 06:00”). During quiet hours a person shows a moon 🌙 to the people watching them; turning it off shows “alert clock stays active”.

5. Connect with someone

How connecting works right now: knokkeroo is not sending invitation messages at the moment. So the person you want to connect with should install knokkeroo first. Then one of you adds the other by phone number, and the other person accepts the request inside the app. Best done with another tester.
  • Tap ＋ Add Contact, enter the other person’s name and mobile number, and send the request.
    Expected: a “Request sent” confirmation; they appear on your screen as a pending request (“Request sent”).
  • On the other person’s phone, open the request from you.
    Expected: the request clearly shows who is asking — your name and number — with a reminder to only accept if they recognise you. (If you don’t recognise a request, you should Decline it.)
  • Have them tap Accept.
    Expected: you now watch over each other — each of you appears as an approved contact on the other’s screen (you may need to pull to refresh).

6. Contacts, colours & their number

Once you have a contact, their tile shows a colour for how recently they checked in:

  • Green — checked in recently.
  • Amber — due to check in.
  • Red — overdue; you may want to reach out.
  • Have your contact tap knok, then refresh your screen.
    Expected: their tile goes green and the time updates.
  • Tap a contact to open Contact settings.
    Expected: you see their phone number (you can’t edit it) so you can check they are who you expect, plus a contact name only you see and an alert interval you can change.

7. Remove a contact

  • Open a contact → Contact settings → Remove contact → confirm.
    Expected: the contact disappears for you, and after a refresh you also disappear from theirs (it’s removed both ways). Neither of you is notified. You can reconnect later by adding each other again.

8. Your plan

knokkeroo will offer a paid subscription so people can watch over more than four contacts. As an early tester you are on a free-forever plan, so you are not limited and should keep seeing all of your contacts. There is nothing you need to buy to test the app.

9. Delete your account (optional — do this last)

Heads up: this permanently deletes your knokkeroo account and data. Only do it if you are finished testing or want to test this feature — you can always sign up again afterwards.
  • Tap Me → Delete my account and confirm.
    Expected: your account is deleted, you are signed out, and you disappear from your contacts’ lists.
