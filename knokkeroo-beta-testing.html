Explanation & tester instructions

What knokkeroo does, and what should happen when you use it

knokkeroo is a private check-in app for trusted contacts. The main action is called a knok. A knok is not a message, not a medical alert, and not a location update. It is a manual check-in that says: I was able to respond now.

In the simplest terms: open knokkeroo, tap knok, and your latest check-in should update. Your approved contacts can then see that you checked in recently.

The app is designed for everyday reassurance between trusted people: family members, friends, older parents and adult children, people living alone, or anyone who wants a low-effort way to say “I’m okay” without needing a phone call.

New in this test build — please try these:

Connecting with someone: the app is not sending invitation messages right now. To connect, both people install knokkeroo, then one of you adds the other by phone number — the other person sees a request inside the app to accept. So if you want a friend or family member to connect, just tell them directly to install knokkeroo first.

Knowing who is asking: when someone asks to connect with you, the app now shows you their name and number. Only accept if you recognise the person and the number — if you don't, tap Decline. Please check this looks right.

Seeing a contact's number: open any contact and you'll now see their phone number on the settings screen (you can't change it). It's there so you can confirm a contact is really who you think it is.

Subscriptions (coming): knokkeroo will offer a paid plan to watch over more than four contacts. As an early tester you are on a free-forever plan, so you will not be limited — you should keep seeing all of your contacts.

What the colours mean

People you are connected to appear as simple status tiles. The colours are there to help you quickly understand whether someone has checked in recently or may need a human follow-up.

Green Checked in recently. This usually means the person has tapped knok within the expected time.
Amber Due to check in. This means the person may soon need to knok again, depending on their settings.
Red Overdue. This means the person has not checked in within the expected time, so you may want to reach out.
👆 Tapping knok

When you tap knok, the app should record a fresh check-in. You should expect your latest check-in time, status, or tile to update so that it is clear something changed.

Tester expectation: after tapping knok, you should not be left wondering whether the tap worked. If the screen does not clearly show that your check-in was recorded, please report that.

🧑‍🤝‍🧑 Approved contacts

A contact should only become trusted after the approval flow is complete. Adding someone by phone number should not automatically make them a trusted contact.

Tester expectation: pending invites, approval requests, accepted contacts, declined contacts, and cancelled invites should be visibly different enough to understand.

🌙 The moon

The moon represents quiet-hours or sleep-time context. It is there to help explain that a person may be inside a period where check-in expectations are treated differently.

Tester expectation: if the moon is shown, it should make sense in relation to the person’s quiet-hours or sleep/wake settings. It should not make the status more confusing.

⚙️ Settings

Settings control how knokkeroo interprets check-ins. When you change settings, the app should save the change and the visible behaviour should match the new setting.

Tester expectation: after changing a setting, return to the relevant screen and check whether the app reflects the new value consistently.

Settings to test carefully

Check-in timing

If the app lets you change how often a person is expected to check in, the displayed status should follow that setting. A shorter interval should make check-ins become due sooner. A longer interval should give more time before the person appears due or overdue.

Quiet hours / sleep and wake times

Quiet hours are intended to stop normal sleep time from being treated like normal active time. If you change sleep or wake times, the app should save them and use the new times when showing status, due, overdue, or moon context.

Timezone handling

Each person’s check-in expectations should make sense in their own local time. If someone is in another country or timezone, their quiet-hours context should not be treated as if they are in your timezone.

Display name and profile details

If you change your display name or profile details, the new information should appear consistently where contacts need to recognise you. Old names should not keep appearing in important places.

Invites and approvals

Inviting someone should show as pending until the other person responds. Accepting, declining, cancelling, or removing should move the contact into the correct state.

Removing a contact

If you remove a contact or cancel an invite, that person should stop appearing as an active approved contact for you. The app should not leave behind a confusing old trusted-contact state.

Useful tester scenarios

  • Sign in: enter your mobile number, complete verification, and confirm that you reach the main app without needing a password.
  • Tap knok: tap the main knok button and check whether your latest check-in visibly updates.
  • Check your own tile: confirm that your own status makes sense after you have checked in.
  • Add a contact: add someone by phone number and check whether they appear as pending until approval is complete.
  • Approve or decline: respond to a contact request and check whether the resulting state is clear, and that the request clearly shows who is asking (name and number).
  • Check a contact's number: open one of your contacts and confirm their phone number is shown on the settings screen so you can verify who they are.
  • Watch colours: look at green, amber, and red states and check whether the meaning is obvious.
  • Look for the moon: check whether the moon appears only where quiet-hours or sleep-time context makes sense.
  • Change settings: update check-in timing, quiet hours, sleep/wake times, or display details and confirm that the app saves and reflects the changes.
  • Remove or cancel: cancel an invite or remove a contact and confirm that the app no longer treats that person as active and approved.
  • Report confusion: if you expected something different from what happened, please tell us exactly what you expected and what actually happened.

Important: knokkeroo is a personal check-in and communication tool. It is not a medical device, alarm, emergency service, healthcare app, location tracker, or substitute for professional or emergency help. The current app version does not send automatic push notifications. If someone is in real danger, call your local emergency services.

Join the test

Pick your phone below and follow the steps carefully.

📱 android testers

Android has two compulsory steps. Please use the two buttons below in order.

The first button joins the knokkeroo Google tester group. That Google Group is the list Google uses to decide who is allowed to test the Android app. Anyone who is accepted into that group is eligible to test knokkeroo on Google Play.

The second button accepts the actual Google Play test invite. Use the same Google/Gmail account for both steps, otherwise Google Play may say that the app is not available or that you are not a tester.

  1. Use an Android phone.
    Check which Google/Gmail account is signed into the Play Store on that phone: open Play Store, tap your profile picture at the top right, and note the email shown there. Use that exact same Gmail address for the next steps.
  2. Step 1 — join the testers group first.
    Open https://groups.google.com/g/knokkeroo-testers and tap Ask to join, or accept the invite if you were already added. This adds your Google account to the tester group that is allowed to access the Android test.
  3. Step 2 — accept the Google Play test invite second.
    Open https://play.google.com/apps/testing/com.goliontus.knokker and tap Become a tester or Accept. This tells Google Play that you want to join the app test.
  4. Install the app.
    On the same page, tap Download it on Google Play, or open https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.goliontus.knokker, then tap Install.
  5. If it says “not available” or “you’re not a tester”.
    This is usually because Step 1 and Step 2 were not both completed with the same Google account, or because Google has not updated access yet. Check the Play Store account on the phone, wait an hour or two, and try again.

🍎 iOS testers

Join on iPhone with TestFlight

iOS testing runs through Apple TestFlight. Use the public invite link below to join the knokkeroo beta and install the test build.

  1. Use an iPhone.
    Make sure you can sign in with the Apple ID you normally use for App Store downloads.
  2. Install TestFlight.
    Open the App Store and install the free TestFlight app from Apple.
  3. Open the knokkeroo TestFlight invite.
    Open https://testflight.apple.com/join/XaabanK9 and tap Accept.
  4. Install the beta.
    In TestFlight, tap Install next to knokkeroo.
  5. If the link says the beta is not available.
    The build may still be waiting for Apple approval, propagating, or the tester limit may have been reached. Please try again later or email us.

🟥 Huawei testers

Join on a Huawei phone (AppGallery)

This is for Huawei phones that use AppGallery instead of Google Play. If your Huawei phone does have Google Play, it is easier to just follow the Android steps above.

Huawei testing works differently. There is no public join link. Huawei only lets us add a tester by their Huawei ID — the email address or mobile number on your Huawei account. So the way in is: you send us your Huawei ID, we add you, and Huawei then sends you an invitation to install from AppGallery.

You will need: a Huawei / HarmonyOS phone with the AppGallery app, a Huawei ID, and your Huawei ID Country/Region set to South Africa or Nigeria (in AppGallery → Me → Settings → Country/Region). If your region is set elsewhere, the test app will not appear for you.

  1. Find your Huawei ID.
    Open AppGallery → tap Me. Note the email address or mobile number your Huawei account uses, and check that your Country/Region is South Africa or Nigeria.
  2. Send us your Huawei ID.
    Send it via our Contact us page with the email or mobile number on your Huawei account and say you would like to test on Huawei. We add you to the AppGallery tester list.
  3. Accept the invitation.
    Huawei sends you an invitation by email or SMS. Open it and accept — this enrols you in the test.
  4. Install from AppGallery.
    After accepting, open knokkeroo in AppGallery and install it like any normal app. Make sure you are signed in to AppGallery with the same Huawei ID you sent us.
  5. If you can't see the app.
    Check that you are signed in with the exact Huawei ID you sent us, that your Country/Region is South Africa or Nigeria, and that a little time has passed since we added you. If it still doesn't appear, email us.
