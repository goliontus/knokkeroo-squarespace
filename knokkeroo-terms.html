2.1 What status indicators mean

Green, amber, and red status indicators reflect only the elapsed time since a user last voluntarily performed a check-in within the app, adjusted for any quiet hours configured for that user. These indicators do not represent any assessment of a person's physical health, mental health, safety, sleep state, location, or current activity.

2.2 Quiet-hours indicator

The quiet-hours indicator, including any moon symbol shown on a contact's status card, reflects only the time window that the relevant user has configured as their preferred quiet period. It does not indicate that the user is asleep, at home, unavailable, safe, unsafe, or in any particular physical state.

2.3 Emergency situations

If a check-in is missed, late, unclear, or concerning, use ordinary judgement and direct human follow-up. If you believe there is immediate risk, contact emergency services immediately.

2.4 Connected devices and wearables

Some versions of knokkeroo let you check in from a paired companion device — for example an Apple Watch, Apple TV, Wear OS watch, or Garmin watch — instead of the phone app directly.

The time recorded for a check-in from a connected device is the time you performed the action on that device (for example, the moment you pressed the check-in button), not the time our servers happened to receive it. If the device has no connectivity at that moment — no Bluetooth connection to your phone, no Wi-Fi, no cellular, depending on the device and your settings — some devices save the check-in and send it once connectivity returns, reporting the original time; other devices or app versions may not, and may require you to try again once connectivity is available. If a reported check-in time is somehow in the future — for example due to a device clock error or an attempt to falsify a check-in — we disregard it and record the actual time our servers received the request instead.

In areas without reception, Bluetooth range, Wi-Fi, or an internet connection — including remote, rural, or wilderness areas — a check-in from a connected device may be delayed, and on some devices or app versions may never be recorded at all. Do not treat pressing a button on a connected device as confirmation that your check-in was received. If you are relying on knokkeroo in an area where connectivity is uncertain, confirm on the device's own screen (or the knokkeroo app once you have connectivity) that the check-in actually succeeded, and do not assume it worked simply because you pressed a button.

3.2 The service and its purpose

knokkeroo is a personal welfare check-in application. It lets users perform voluntary check-ins and share their check-in status with approved contacts. Contacts watch over each other through mutual, both-approved connections. A connection can be removed at any time, which removes it for both parties.

knokkeroo is intended for voluntary welfare check-ins between trusted, approved contacts. A check-in means only that the user has chosen to indicate they are okay; it must not be used as a test of affection, loyalty, obedience, availability, or relationship commitment.

For privacy and anti-abuse reasons, knokkeroo may not disclose whether an invitation message was delivered, whether a phone number is reachable, whether a person already has a knokkeroo account, or whether a person has viewed an invitation, unless that person chooses to accept or approve the connection.
